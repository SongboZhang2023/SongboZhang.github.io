Finite-momentum Cooper pairing in proximitized altermagnets
PDF
P. M. Lenggenhager, J. Maciejko, and T. Bzdušek
S.-B. Zhang, Lun-Hui Hu, and T. Neupert
arXiv:2302.13185 (2023)
 – 
Published
February 2023

Finite-momentum Cooper pairing is an unconventional form of superconductivity which is widely believed to require a finite magnetization. Altermagnetism is an emergent magnetic phase with highly anisotropic spin-splitting of specific symmetries but zero net magnetization. Here, we study Cooper pairing in an altermagnetic metal in proximity to conventional $s$-wave superconductors. Remarkably, we find that the Cooper pairs induced in the altermagnet acquire a finite centre-of-mass momentum, despite the zero net magnetization in the system. This Cooper-pair momentum is strongly dependent on the direction of Cooper-pair propagation and exhibits unusual symmetric patterns. Furthermore, it yields several unique features: (i) The order parameter oscillates with the doping in the altermagnet and/or with the distance from the superconductor, which is sensitively dependent on the junction orientation; (ii) In planar Josephson junctions, although there is no net magnetization, 0-$\pi$ transitions occur as a function of doping in the altermagnet, length or orientation of the junction; (iii) In junctions parallel to the direction where the spin splitting of the altermagnet vanishes, the superconducting transport is dominated by Cooper pairs moving at large oblique angles away from the junction direction, n effect not seen in any ferromagnetic or antiferromagnetic counterpart. Finally, we discuss the implementation of our predictions in candidate materials such as RuO2 and KRu4O8.

Optimal Renormalization Group Transformation from Information Theory

P. M. Lenggenhager, D. E. Gökmen, Z. Ringel, S. D. Huber, and M. Koch-Janusz
Phys. Rev. X 10, 011037 (2020)
 – 
Published
14 Feb 2020

We consider a recently introduced real-space renormalization group (RG) algorithm which is based on maximizing real-space mutual information (RSMI). Based on general proofs and detailed studies of arbitrary coarse-grainings of the Ising chain, we argue that maximizing RSMI minimizes the range of interactions and, for disordered systems, correlations in the disorder distribution of the coarse-grained system.
